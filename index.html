About IEEE Photonics Society

The IEEE Photonics Society serves as the hub of a vibrant technical community, comprising over 100,000 professionals dedicated to transforming breakthroughs in quantum physics into innovative devices, systems, and products that revolutionize daily life. The society's impact is evident in various aspects, including global communications via fiber optics, medical applications of lasers, flat-screen displays, photovoltaic devices for solar energy, and LEDs for energy-efficient illumination. To facilitate knowledge sharing and dissemination, the society organizes, contributes to, and participates in technical conferences, journals, and activities covering all aspects of photonics. As a vital part of IEEE, the world's largest technical professional association, the society provides its members with opportunities for professional growth, publishes journals, sponsors conferences, and supports local chapters and student activities worldwide.

About IIITDM, Kancheepuram

The Indian Institute of Information Technology Design and Manufacturing Kancheepuram (IIITDM Kancheepuram) is a Centre of Excellence for technical education and research established in 2007 by the Ministry of Education, Government of India. The institute aims to pursue design and manufacturing oriented engineering education and research, and to promote the competitive advantage of Indian products in global markets. IIITDM Kancheepuram offers academic and research programs that integrate engineering design, manufacturing, and management with information technology.

The institute offers undergraduate and postgraduate programs in Computer Engineering, Electronics and Communication Engineering, Mechanical Engineering and interdisciplinary Ph.D. programs. The curricula are designed in collaboration with IITs, IISc, and industry stakeholders to provide solutions to current technological challenges. Graduates from IIITDM Kancheepuram can find opportunities in IT and IT-enabled product companies, automotive design, consumer product design and manufacturing, and knowledge/service-based industries. With a strong foundation in design, engineering skills, and managerial expertise, graduates can also pursue entrepreneurship in design and manufacturing.
